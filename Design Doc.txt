Refactors
🧱 1. Game Setup / Initialization
Move to: game_setup.py or initializer.py

init_pygame()

load_images()

load_sounds()

Any pygame.font, pygame.mixer, or pygame.display setup

✅ Benefit: Keeps your main loop focused on game logic, not bootstrapping.

🧠 2. Event Handling
Move to: input_handler.py

The entire for event in pygame.event.get(): block, especially:

Mouse clicks

Keypresses

Spell interactions

UI button triggers

✅ Benefit: Makes the main loop readable, and event logic testable.

🧠 3. Game State Transitions
Move to: state_manager.py

Transition logic like:

Switching between menus, quest selection, and board states

Restarting game

Winning/losing/ending animations

✅ Benefit: Clean separation of gameplay logic vs. state machine behavior.

✨ 4. Animation Control
Move to: animations.py

Control timers

Shake effects

Pulse logic

Card entry/exit transitions

Meteor/quadrant flash cycle

✅ Benefit: Can reuse animation patterns across UI, board, cards, etc.


🃏 6. Quest Selection / Handling
Move to: quest_screen.py

draw_quest_selection()

check_quest_win()

win_quest()

Card drawing/hover/click logic

✅ Benefit: All quest card logic lives in one place—easy to evolve.

📦 7. Save/Load or JSON I/O
Move to: save_system.py or data_loader.py

Anything touching quests.json, game config, or save slots

9. Last-Pawn-Standing
Objective: Finish the game with exactly one pawn of either color remaining on the board.

Reward: Pawn Clone - copy any pawn (with its rank) to the adjacent file.

Difficulty: Medium; requires endgame finesse.

Flavor: “When only one seed survives, plant another.”

10. Friendly Fire Drill
Objective: Capture one of your own pieces by bomb or swap effect.

Reward: Advanced Shield Kit - place a 2-turn invincible bubble on any 2×2 zone.

Difficulty: Easy-medium.

Flavor: “Pain is the best rehearsal.”

11. Silent Spring
Objective: Deliver checkmate using only bishops and pawns.

Reward: Forest-Snare - root every enemy piece on dark squares for one full turn.

Difficulty: Hard; thematic for green-zone wizard.

Flavor: “The quiet growl of nature claims all.”

12. Knight School
Objective: Fork king and queen twice in one game (need not capture).

Reward: Swap Steed - instantly trade positions of any two friendly pieces.

Difficulty: Skill-based, flashy.

Flavor: “A good horse attends two classes at once.”

13. Castled Doom
Objective: Castle, then be checkmated without ever moving that rook.

Reward: Retractable Rampart - next game begin with rook already on f-file behind a pawn wall.

Difficulty: Medium-hard, usually concedes a loss.

Flavor: “Fortress or tomb—walls remember.”

14. Sphinx’s Riddle
Objective: Make exactly 42 moves total (both players) before delivering mate or resigning.

Reward: Wildcard Voucher - pick any unlocked item you don’t own yet.

Difficulty: Precision puzzle; good for advanced players.

Flavor: “Answer life, the universe, and everything—in moves.”

15. Ghost Town
Objective: Have at least four pieces on the board that cannot move (pinned or frozen) at once.

Reward: Mass Phasing - turn all friendly pieces ethereal for one move (can pass through but not capture).

Difficulty: Moderate; encourages odd positions.

Flavor: “Stillness breeds the uncanny.”

16. Storm Warning
Objective: Place your king on three different files while in check during one game (escape tour).

Reward: Static Discharge - shock all enemy pawns; they skip their next move.

Difficulty: High drama but doable.

Flavor: “Lightning never strikes once.”

17. Reverse Psychology
Objective: Promote an enemy pawn (let it reach the back rank) before promoting any of yours.

Reward: Corrupted Crown - steal the highest-value enemy piece for two turns.

Difficulty: Medium; risky but stylish.

Flavor: “Hand them a crown, steal their throne.”

18. Tunnel Vision
Objective: Make 15 consecutive moves with the same piece.

Reward: Burrow Drill - carve a straight tunnel: move any piece to the square directly in front, capturing all in-between.

Difficulty: Situational; easier with a queen.

Flavor: “When one path is clear, dig deeper.”

19. Double or Nothing
Objective: Offer a draw twice and have both declined, then win.

Reward: Second Chance Token - replay one lost game in the set immediately.

Difficulty: Social / AI-tuning dependent; rare.

Flavor: “Fortune favors the stubborn.”

20. Checkpoint Charlie
Objective: Be in check on exactly five separate turns before move 25, then escape and win.

Reward: Checkpoint Rewind - mark a board state; if things go south later, revert to it once.

Difficulty: High; “rope-a-dope” style.

Flavor: “Dance on the edge, then own the stage.”




21. The King’s Pilgrimage
Objective: Move your king to the 8th rank (opponent’s back row).

Reward: King’s Mantle - start your next game with the king shielded for 5 turns.

Difficulty: High risk, highly thematic.

Flavor: “He walked into the enemy's throne room—unbent, unbowed, unbroken.”

22. Blood Pact
Objective: Lose three pawns in the first 10 moves.

Reward: Berserker’s Blade - all your pawns gain the ability to capture forward for 3 turns.

Difficulty: Medium; may unbalance your structure.

Flavor: “Their blood paves the path to fury.”

23. The Long Con
Objective: Win with your last remaining piece.

Reward: Shadow Cloak - one friendly piece becomes invisible for 5 turns.

Difficulty: High; must survive with a solo piece.

Flavor: “They thought you were done. You were just getting started.”

Reward: Ice Crown - freeze all diagonally adjacent enemy pieces for one turn.

Difficulty: Easy-medium depending on queen placement.

Flavor: “Some queens rule without lifting a finger.”

25. Reckless Endangerment
Objective: Place your king adjacent to an enemy piece and survive for two full turns.

Reward: Divine Intervention - cancel the next check or mate. Once per g.

Difficulty: High stakes but controlled risk.

Flavor: “He stood face to face with death… and lived.”

26. Pawn Storm

Reward: Pawnquake - all pawns move forward one rank instantly (including enemies).

Difficulty: Medium.

Flavor: “A wall of grain rolls like thunder.”

Difficulty: Medium; sometimes natural.

Flavor: “The house always wins—unless you are the house.”

Reward: Time Mirror - repeat the exact move you made one turn ago, regardless of legality.

Difficulty: Easy in short games, harder in endgames.

Flavor: “Some things never change. Some should.”

29. The Interloper
Objective: Get an enemy piece onto your back rank (without capturing it).

Reward: Trap Door - remove any one enemy piece from the board instantly.

Difficulty: Medium; requires letting a piece through.

Flavor: “Let them in… just to pull the rug.”

30. Knightmare
Objective: Move a single knight 10+ times in one g.

Reward: Horned Mount - one piece can leap like a knight for a turn.

Difficulty: Low-medium.

Flavor: “The night belongs to the restless.”

Objective: Put your king between two enemy pieces that could capture diagonally (but don’t).

Reward: Arcane Barrier - next time your king would be checked from two sides, negate both.

Difficulty: Risky and specific.

Flavor: “The eye of the storm is still—until it isn't.”

32. The Martyr's Game
Objective: Lose four pieces in four turns and still win the g.

Reward: Martyr’s Medal - one piece explodes on death, taking adjacent enemies.

Difficulty: High; aggressive trade-off.

Flavor: “They died so you could win.”

Difficulty: Requires the right item in hand.

Flavor: “They never saw it coming—not even from space.”

Difficulty: High-skill.

Flavor: “Layer upon layer of terror.”

Difficulty: Variable; comeback-driven.

Flavor: “The tide never asks for permission.”

Difficulty: Reactive, tactical.

Flavor: “You take mine—I become yours.”

37. Backline Brawl

Reward: Siege Engine - turn one pawn into a rook midgame.

Difficulty: Medium-hard.

Flavor: “Behind enemy lines, walls fall fastest.”

Flavor: “A world without order births monsters.”

Reward: Royal Token - one pawn starts promoted in your next g.

Difficulty: Board-state dependent, easier in midgame.

Flavor: “Power flows where structure holds.”
